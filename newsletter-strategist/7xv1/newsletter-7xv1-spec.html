Newsletter Structure Overview

Each email is built from white content cards stacked on a warm cream (#FEF5E8) background inside a 670px container. Below is the full section flow with content type indicated.

Monetization

7 Location Report
Weather → ad slot → house listing
8 Sign Off
Cait Baker signature + referral CTA
9 Footer
Logo + disclosure + copyright
Content pipeline: Scripts + scraping + AI → Airtable → beehiiv API. All content is automated except affiliate copy (written manually).
6 fixed monetization slots per edition. Placements are identical across all 511+ locations — not geo-targeted. Items use the .affiliate CSS class for identification.
Daily event themes: Mon = best of week, Tue = free events, Wed = family, Thu = adults only, Fri = weekend preview, Sat = weekend events, Sun = next week preview + best-of recap across all sections.
"Location Report" (not "City Report") because coverage includes counties and areas, not just cities. Heading is location-specific (e.g. "Tacoma report", "Pierce County report").
Send schedule: 7 days/week starting March 2 (previously 4x/week). Platform: beehiiv. Tracking: ClickMagick (website), beehiiv (email).

Monetization Map

4 placement types per edition

Every edition has 4 monetization placements (Lead Sponsor or Intro, In the News, Highlights, We Got Your Back). Identical across all locations — not geo-targeted. Affiliate copy is the only manually written content.

1Lead Sponsor
Two-part placement that works as a pair (when booked):
(a) Intro paragraph in Section 1
(b) Dedicated card in Section 3
1aIntro
Affiliate item in intro area (when no Lead Sponsor)
2-3 sentences woven into greeting copy.
2In the News
2 affiliate items after 3 organic items
Bold headline + summary + link. First-person editorial style.
3Highlights
0-2 bullet items after 3 organic bullets
Short one-liner with linked phrase.
4We Got Your Back
~5 items per edition (fully monetized)
Same format as In the News items.
HTML tagging: All monetization items use the .affiliate CSS class for identification and tracking.
