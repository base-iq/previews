The Fed signals its first rate cut of the year. Savers have a narrow window.

Certificate rates tend to fall before the announcement does. What to lock in, and when.

Federal Reserve officials emerged from Tuesday's meeting pointing, for the first time this year, toward a September cut in the benchmark rate. Markets put the odds above 80 percent by Tuesday evening. For borrowers, relief arrives slowly; mortgage rates have already priced in much of the move. For savers, the timing runs the other way.

Banks historically trim their certificate and high-yield savings offers four to six weeks ahead of a cut, which puts the best of the current 5-percent CDs on borrowed time. Anyone sitting on idle cash has roughly a month to lock a rate that may not return for years — read the full story.

Across Ohio

Property-tax relief comes to a vote: The Ohio House votes Thursday on a homestead expansion that would freeze property-tax bills for homeowners 65 and older earning under $70,000. County auditors estimate 830,000 households would qualify — what the freeze would mean for yours.
State fair opens July 29: The Ohio State Fair runs twelve days this year with admission holding at $10 for visitors 60 and up. Advance ride wristbands went on sale Monday and sold out in 2024, so early is wise — tickets and the daily schedule.
I-75 lane closures through August: ODOT begins resurfacing the 18-mile stretch between Middletown and Franklin next week, with overnight single-lane traffic in both directions. The agency posted detour maps and a completion calendar — plan around the work.
