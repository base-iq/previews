Top10Today — Redesign directions, round 3 · July 16, 2026

Clean, simple, straightforward

The recalibration: this is a news product for readers 50 and older, and rounds 1–2 kept building shrines to the number 10 — arches, seals, tallies, tickets, podiums. All gone. These five are flat typographic mastheads, classic readable type at generous sizes, one restrained accent apiece, and numerals that are just numerals. The countdown lives in the content, not the logo. The differences between variations are temperature and typographic voice — nothing here needs decoding.

What every variation keeps

  • The countdown. Every ranked list descends; rank 1 renders last.
  • A quiet finale. Intro stamp, accent numeral, bigger headline — emphasis by weight and size, never by device.
  • Paid placements are ranked items. Asterisk register, disclosure in the fine print.
  • Readability first. 16.5–17px body at 1.6+ line height, AA+ contrast on all working text, 44px touch targets, aligned rank columns for scanning.
  • One accent, budgeted. Each variation lists exactly where its accent may appear. Everything else is ink on paper.

Reading this round: the five differ on temperature (espresso-warm → slate-cool), type voice (bookish → newsprint → friendly), and accent (toffee / none / plum / slate / rosewood) — but they're all the same calm product. Mixing is expected: "J's layout with K's plum," "I but with L's fonts" — any combination builds cleanly into the consolidated proposal. Rounds 1 and 2 remain up for reference. Brain, logos, and the territory table still untouched.
