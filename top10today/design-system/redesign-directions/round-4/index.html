Variation P

Crisper serif

Same layout as N, but Source Serif 4 replaces Merriweather everywhere. Merriweather is woolly and bookish; Source Serif is lighter on its feet — sharper serifs, more air. The page feels about 10% more modern without losing the news register.

Variation Q

All sans

No serif anywhere — Inter for numerals, headlines, and body. The most contemporary reading of The Bulletin: a briefing, not a paper. Hierarchy comes entirely from weight and size.

Variation R

Compact

N's faces, tighter rhythm: smaller numerals, closer rows, 15px blurbs. Ten items fit in roughly two-thirds the scroll. Built for the reader who scans the whole list before breakfast.

Variation S

Pure ink

Even the slate goes. Links are ink, underlined — the underline alone marks them. One addition so the brand still has a signature: a 4px ink band across the very top of every surface, like a bulletin board's frame.

Fixed across all variations

  • The Bulletin's core: ink on white, flat typographic masthead, aligned numeral column, quiet weight-and-size finale, hairline rules.
  • The countdown. Descending, rank 1 last, paid items ranked with the asterisk register.
  • Readability floor. AA+ contrast, 44px touch targets, 1.6 line height (R's compact body is the one deliberate exception to test).

These five compose: masthead (N/O), serif (N/P/Q), density (default/R), color (slate/S) are independent choices — "O's nameplate + P's serif + S's pure ink" is a valid single answer.
