Three new looks for the countdown

The brief: replace the colors, the logo, and the fonts. Everything structural stays — every list still counts down to #1, the finale still gets the staging, paid placements are still ranked items, the dark band still frames the page. Each direction below is a complete, different answer.

A — The Velvet Rope B — The Hot List C — The Tally

Territory constraint: the four sibling brands lock away warm terracotta (BaseLocal), ink navy + crimson (AmericanAdvisor), black + wire cobalt (LocalUpdate), and marigold sunrise (TodayLocal) — and this redesign vacates midnight teal + mint. Each direction claims a genuinely free corner of the portfolio and notes its nearest neighbor.

Direction A

The Velvet Rope

Awards night. Deep curtain violet and gilt champagne — the countdown as a black-tie ceremony, and the mark is a proscenium arch: the numeral standing on stage. Elegant where the current identity is broadcast-casual.

The mark — the proscenium

The numeral 10 in Fraunces stands inside a stage arch — rounded at the top like a theater proscenium, squared at the feet like a doorway you're ushered through. On dark: gilt arch and numeral. On light: the arch goes deep violet. Rank numerals in the countdown live in small arches; the finale is the one filled arch.

Why it's not the ring: the ring said spotlight; the arch says stage. Same theater, different seat — closer to the velvet-curtain end of the register.

Direction B

The mark — the burst seal

A twenty-point burst holding the 10 in Alfa Slab One — the sticker on the window, the seal on the winner. On dark: blush burst, ink numeral. On light: raspberry burst, white numeral. Ranked items use bare slab numerals with a raspberry period; the burst itself is reserved for the finale — #1 literally gets the seal of approval.

Why it works for this brand: ranking culture already speaks in badges and seals. This direction leans in — playful-but-credible carried by the drawing being geometric and disciplined, never a clip-art starburst with an exclamation point.

Direction C

The Tally

The scoreboard in the back room — warm press-black ink, one shot of citron, and condensed poster numerals. The mark is ten kept the way humans actually count: two tally clusters. The most graphic, least decorated direction — the constraint is "one color of ink plus one highlighter."

The mark — the tally ten

Two clusters of five — four strokes and the slash — the way a score has been kept on every chalkboard and bar napkin in history. It's a "10" you read instantly without a numeral, and no competitor list-brand has it. One cluster citron, one white: the count in progress. The wordmark is Anton, set like a poster.

At small sizes (favicon, avatar) the mark reduces to a single citron cluster on ink — a "5" to the pedantic, a tally to everyone else; the proposal stage would test this reduction properly.

What every direction keeps

  • The countdown. Every ranked list descends; rank 1 renders last, at every size.
  • The finale staging. One marked #1 per surface — intro stamp, the system's single filled device, the one display-face sentence.
  • Paid placements are ranked items. Asterisk register, disclosure in the fine print, never interruptions between ranks.
  • The frame discipline. The dark ground frames (masthead, finale, footer) and never becomes a page background; the on-dark accent never appears on light.
  • The broadcast voice. Intro stamps, sentence case, headlines that never exclaim. Copy is untouched by this redesign.
  • Accessibility floor. AA+ minimum on all working text, 44px touch targets, motion rationed to one beat.

Next step: pick a direction (or a combination — e.g. A's palette with B's type). The pick becomes a consolidated proposal on this board for a second look; only after that approval do the brand guide, design system, portfolio territory table, and logo assets get rebuilt. The current identity remains untouched until then.
