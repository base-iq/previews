3.15 Editorial Lede

First body paragraph after the H1 in editorial-style bridge pages. Larger and lighter than body — earns the H1 its space and pulls the eye into the prose. Used together with Drop Cap (3.16), Article Byline (3.17), and Pull Quote (3.18) when a bridge page takes a long-form magazine treatment.

GLP-1 isn't a celebrity drug anymore. Here's what changed for women over 50.

We've spent a few months looking at where weight-loss medication is actually delivering for our readers. Short version: it's working, the price has dropped to something normal, and most women starting it are over 45.

3.16 Drop Cap

Visual entry point on the first paragraph of an editorial article. One per article maximum. Reserved for editorial bridge pages — never on standard sales bridges or short content.

A reader emailed us last fall with a very direct question: was the weight-loss medication everyone was talking about something she should bother looking into, or was it another thing she'd waste a year on. She was 58. She'd done Weight Watchers twice, Noom, the keto thing, and most recently a meal-kit subscription her daughter swore by. Nothing stuck.

3.17 Article Byline

Between the H1+lede block and the article body on editorial bridge pages. Establishes authorship, freshness, and approximate read time. Stacks left-then-right at ≤480px.

By the BaseLocal team Updated April 2026 · 5 min read

3.18 Pull Quote

Voice-forward amplification of a sentence pulled from the body — breaks the reading rhythm. Different from Stat Callout (3.4), which is data-forward and uses a cream background; this is prose-forward and uses no fill. Optional `<cite>` below for attributed quotes.

The mechanism is the part most readers haven't heard explained calmly. GLP-1 doesn't burn calories or replace meals. It changes one signal — the one between your gut and your brain — that tends to start shouting at you somewhere around 50.

The price has dropped from $1,000 a month to under $200, and the women using it most aren't celebrities. They're our readers.

Fix the signal, and the rest tends to get easier — most of our readers see meaningful change in the first month.

Variant — with cite

I lost 32 pounds without thinking about food the way I used to. That was the change.
— Linda, 61, BaseLocal reader (Phoenix)

4.13Confirmation Block

Done-state hero. Big green check, headline, body, optional next-steps callout.

✓

You're in.

Your event has been submitted and your payment has been processed. We'll review the details and get it live in the directory within one business day.

What happens next

  • You'll get an email confirmation with your order number and a receipt.
  • Our team reviews the submission for fit and accuracy — usually within one business day.
  • Once approved, your event is pinned to the top of the directory in each location you picked, on the days you picked.
  • If you chose Directory + Newsletter, your event also runs inside the newsletter on those days.
