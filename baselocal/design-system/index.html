3.15 Editorial Lede

First body paragraph after the H1 in editorial-style bridge pages. Larger and lighter than body — earns the H1 its space and pulls the eye into the prose. Used together with Drop Cap (3.16), Article Byline (3.17), and Pull Quote (3.18) when a bridge page takes a long-form magazine treatment.

GLP-1 isn't a celebrity drug anymore. Here's what changed for women over 50.

We've spent a few months looking at where weight-loss medication is actually delivering for our readers. Short version: it's working, the price has dropped to something normal, and most women starting it are over 45.

3.16 Drop Cap

Visual entry point on the first paragraph of an editorial article. One per article maximum. Reserved for editorial bridge pages — never on standard sales bridges or short content.

A reader emailed us last fall with a very direct question: was the weight-loss medication everyone was talking about something she should bother looking into, or was it another thing she'd waste a year on. She was 58. She'd done Weight Watchers twice, Noom, the keto thing, and most recently a meal-kit subscription her daughter swore by. Nothing stuck.

3.17 Article Byline

Between the H1+lede block and the article body on editorial bridge pages. Establishes authorship, freshness, and approximate read time. Stacks left-then-right at ≤480px.

3.18 Pull Quote

Voice-forward amplification of a sentence pulled from the body — breaks the reading rhythm. Different from Stat Callout (3.4), which is data-forward and uses a cream background; this is prose-forward and uses no fill. Optional `<cite>` below for attributed quotes.

The mechanism is the part most readers haven't heard explained calmly. GLP-1 doesn't burn calories or replace meals. It changes one signal — the one between your gut and your brain — that tends to start shouting at you somewhere around 50.

The price has dropped from $1,000 a month to under $200, and the women using it most aren't celebrities. They're our readers.

Fix the signal, and the rest tends to get easier — most of our readers see meaningful change in the first month.

Variant — with cite

I lost 32 pounds without thinking about food the way I used to. That was the change.

3.21 Front-Matter Divider

Short centered horizontal rule (`<hr class="article-divider">`). Sits between the hero (kicker → meta → H1 → lede) and the article body on editorial bridge pages. 64px wide, 1px Warm Border, 40px vertical margins. Distinct from Section Divider (3.14) which is full-width and used between body sections — this one signals the front-matter-to-body break only.

3.22 Article Meta

Small inline metadata line that sits between the kicker and the H1 on editorial bridge pages. Lato 400 13px Silver. Format: "Updated {Month Year} · {N} min read". 3.23 Feature Comparison Table

An N-column (3-up) product feature matrix for bridge / comparison pages — services as columns, features as rows, one column highlighted as the BaseLocal pick (terracotta top-accent + cream tint). Cells hold a green ✓ ("included"), a muted ✗ ("not offered"), or short text ("420+", "Up to $5M"). The row-label column freezes on the left during horizontal scroll on mobile; an optional footer row carries a per-service CTA. Sits between the Comparison Box / VS Box (3.5, two-column text) and Price Comparison (3.20, two-card pricing) — use it when 3+ options need a side-by-side feature read.

Color note: the ✓/✗ glyphs use Green (success) and Silver (muted) — not terracotta. Terracotta is held back for the featured-column accent and the CTA, so the recommendation stays the loudest thing in the component.

3.24 Offer Card

A compact, tagline-led product card for comparison / bridge pages — meant to sit below a Feature Comparison Table (3.23) as the act-on-it layer, after the table has done the at-a-glance work. Lighter than the editorial Pick Cards (3.8 / 3.9): a name, a one-line tagline, a row of feature chips instead of a checkmark list, a price line, and one full-width CTA. Cards stack vertically. The featured / recommended card is "circled" — a 2px terracotta border, a soft terracotta glow, and a hand-marked badge tilted off the top edge, like a friend drew a ring around it.

Buttons: the featured card uses a solid Primary button; the others use the outline Secondary button — so the recommended card's CTA is visibly the loudest. Chips marked .on use the success-green affirmative palette (a feature this option is strong on); plain chips are neutral cream. The whole card is optionally clickable (overlay link + lift-on-hover); when it is, the visible CTA renders as a <span class="btn"> and the card carries the link.

A trust row that sits under the hero: a byline on the left (the Article Byline pattern, 3.17 — author bold, date muted) and one or more trust chips on the right (reader reach, edition count, independence). 3.27 Action Callout

A two-step prompt that pairs a problem with its fix: a "see it" step (a single sentence with a quiet inline link) and a "fix it" step (a short explainer ending in a bold link, no button). A terracotta accent bar runs across the top; the two steps sit as equal columns split by a centered divider, each with a small leading icon. On narrow screens (container ≤ 460px) the columns stack and the divider becomes a horizontal rule. The two link treatments differ on purpose: the "see it" link is a quiet underline, the "fix it" link is a bold action link.

5.8 Directory Changelog

Proof-of-maintenance log for curated reference surfaces (directory root; future registries). Day-stamped entries for additions, retirements, and re-checks — the visible evidence that the list is alive. Heading is neutral Gray — the brand color reads as a link on content pages, so non-interactive labels avoid it. Glyph colors follow the affirmative/absence rule: + and ✓ in success Green, – in Silver (a closure is loss, not error — never red), and no Terracotta (reserved for links/CTAs around it). Entries end with a short editorial clause — the voice moment. Empty month → the component is omitted entirely, never a "no changes" filler row. Container carries a stable anchor id (citable).

What changed this month

  • Jul 18+ Sound Plumbing Co. added to Services — readers kept recommending it to each other
  • Jul 14+ Cirice added to Eat & drink — wood-fired everything on 6th Ave
  • Jul 11– Harbor Lights Diner retired — closed after 14 years; we'll miss the hash browns
  • Jul 8✓ All 38 Wellness listings re-checked — hours and contacts verified

Shows the latest 4–6 entries; no archive page. Date column: Lato 700 12px uppercase Gray. Rows: 14px Gray with names in bold Espresso, hairline-divided.

5.9 BaseLocal's Best Badge

The link-earning embed: a business listed in a directory earns the mark and places it on its own site, linked to its listing's fragment URL. Also rendered on directory pages inside the badge-program module. The name is set in Bitter — the plaque register, distinct from UI chrome. Rules: only listed businesses may use it (inclusion is editor-curated, so the claim is defensible under the hype rule); it always links to the listing (followed link — that is the point); no city name in the lockup, no year, no star implication.
