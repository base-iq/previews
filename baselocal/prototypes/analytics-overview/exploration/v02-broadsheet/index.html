Three days into July, $37,400 is on the books. The month needs $12,900 a day from here to reach its $400,000 target; the best day of the last three weeks brought $13,800, so the target is within reach but leaves no slack. The $12,000 daily floor has held on all but one of the last seven days.

June closed at $286,000 — up 35.5 per cent on May, but $4,000 under its own target and the roadmap’s first miss. The lever for July is volume: sends rise from 15 a week to 22 on July 20, and to 25 in August. Rate is doing its part. Revenue per subscriber printed $0.34 for the month to date, up three cents on June and on schedule for the $0.50 the December plan requires — provided August clears its $0.45 guardrail. The offer desk holds $1.86 per click across 34 active offers, though only three of June’s ten test winners have been solidified.

Concentration remains the quiet caveat: Morning Roundup carries 58 per cent of July’s revenue, and the top two products carry four-fifths of it.

Acquisition

The gap between here and $1M is measured in subscribers.

The book stands at 985,000 against a path that calls for 1,290,000 by the end of July. Net growth is pacing 146,000 a month; the plan assumes 200,000 — that is 6,500 a day, against the 4,700 a day July is producing.

The economics are sound: gross adds hit 42,000 in the last full week, welcome revenue per new subscriber has nearly doubled since May to $0.11, and CAC pays back in 41 days. The shortfall is scale, not efficiency — losses ticked to 10,000 a week as volume rose, worth watching through the send expansion.

Engagement

The audience is absorbing more volume without complaint.

Opens have climbed a point since May to 41.2 per cent, click-through sits at 2.1 per cent, and the engaged share reached 54.0 for July to date. Unsubscribes per send crept from 0.27 to 0.31 per cent — the expected cost of volume, watched closely.

Churn at 19.4 per cent leaves headroom under the 22 per cent guardrail. The July 20 send expansion is the real test of that headroom; these five panels are the ones to reread in three weeks.
