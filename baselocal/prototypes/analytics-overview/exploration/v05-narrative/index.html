Act I · Where we are

Will we hit $400k this month?

CLOSE — $386k pace

June closed at $286k against a $290k target — 99% of plan, and up 35.5% on May. July asks for $400k. Through three days we've banked $37.4k, an average of $12.5k a day, and the straight-line projection lands at $386k. Closing the gap means $12.9k a day from here forward.

Act II · What's working

Is the audience staying healthy while we scale?

YES

Everything we watch for audience fatigue is moving the right way, nine weeks running. Engaged share is up from 51.2% to 54.0%. Opens climbed from 40.0% to 41.2%. Click-through went from 1.95% to 2.1%. This is what it looks like when volume grows and the audience doesn't flinch.

Act III · What's at risk

Are subscribers growing fast enough for the $1M month?

NO — 146k pace vs 200k

The December math is unforgiving: $1M a month requires 2M subscribers at $0.50 RPS. We're at 985k. Net adds were 102k in May, 118k in June, and July is pacing to +146k against the 200k a month the path demands. That's 4.7k net a day when we need 6.5k.

Subscriber book — actuals, target path, and today's pace
terracotta: actuals · ticks: monthly target path · dashed: projection at +146k/mo

Hold today's pace and December lands near 1.85M — roughly 150k short of the book the $1M month needs. The gap doesn't show up this month; it compounds as the path steepens through the fall. Gross adds are actually climbing — 31k to 42k a week since May — but weekly losses climbed too, from 7k to 10k.

The second risk hides inside the first: every new subscriber is underpaying on arrival. Welcome flow yields $0.11 per gross new sub against a $0.15 target — up from $0.06 in May, still $0.04 light. At 42k gross adds a week, that gap is real money, and it also stretches CAC payback, currently 41 days.

Act IV · What happens next

Is RPS on the path to $0.50?

MOVING — $0.34

Revenue per subscriber is the lever that makes the December book requirement smaller — and it's moving: $0.29 → $0.31 → $0.34 across May, June, and July-to-date. The path to $0.50 by December needs roughly +$0.04 a month from here. August is the checkpoint: if RPS lands under $0.45, we pause the send expansion and reassess.

Four dated moves carry the second half. Each one feeds either the RPS line above or the subscriber gap in Act III — nothing on this list is decoration.

Jul 20
Send expansion: 15 → 22 emails/week. The single biggest revenue lever for closing July's $14k gap. Watch opens and unsubs through the transition.
Aug 1
Second expansion: 22 → 25 emails/week — conditional on the audience holding. RPS guardrail: stay at or above $0.45 for August or the expansion pauses.
August
Welcome yield $0.11 → $0.15 per gross sub. Closes the arrival-value gap and shortens the 41-day CAC payback, which unlocks more acquisition spend against the 200k/mo target.
July
Solidify the remaining 7 of 10 June offer winners. The cheapest RPS gain on the board — proven offers, just not yet locked into rotation.
The month is winnable, the audience can carry more, and the machine that turns attention into revenue keeps improving. What we don't have yet is enough new readers walking in the door. That's the problem July needs to solve.
