# TIBO Titanium Cutting Board Bridge Page — Implementation Spec

**Brand:** BaseLocal
**Feature slug:** `tibo-titanium-cutting-board` (production URL: `/tibo-titanium-cutting-board/`)
**Prototype:** [https://base-iq.github.io/previews/baselocal/prototypes/tibo-titanium-cutting-board/](https://base-iq.github.io/previews/baselocal/prototypes/tibo-titanium-cutting-board/)
**A/B test:** The **value-angle challenger against the control** (the six-rounds page); it **replaces the earlier `-value` variant**, which was retired. The page is built on a value/price argument rather than a feature scorecard.

> **Attribution:** handled **upstream at the ad platform** (per-bridge click IDs), so the arms intentionally share the same offer URL — no per-page `sub_id`/`utm_term` is added here.

---

## 1. Goal

An advertorial bridge page that presells the TIBO 4-in-1 titanium cutting board before sending traffic to the gettibo.com offer page. It replaces a third-party (Hoom-branded) advertorial in the funnel.

**The argument (story-driven, not a block stack).** The page **reveals first and justifies second**: the H1 renders a verdict and the ad promised "see what they're using instead," so TIBO is named immediately, and the rest of the page earns the reveal. Still **desire before price**, in four beats:

1. **The reveal — the board they picked.** Two short beats directly under the hero name **TIBO's 4-in-1** [link → offer] and inventory the four tools (titanium face, produce side, handle sharpener, grater). Pays off the ad's promise in the first three seconds instead of withholding the product for half the page.
2. **Evidence — what the board leaves in your food.** H2 "What the research says" (receipts for the H1's "the research says they're right"): a side-by-side table compares the three materials across microplastics and bacteria (with study citations, a wood-"naturally antibacterial" myth-bust, and three supporting photos). Titanium is the only material that leaves neither.
3. **Why it's worth it — material and features, in one section.** Beyond keeping food clean, titanium is a premium, lasts-forever material (Grade 2 medical-grade) and a four-tool board shown *in use* (the reveal already inventoried the tools, so this section demonstrates rather than re-lists): flip-and-go surfaces, knives that sharpen instead of dull, plus the verified chef's review. One section (H2 "Titanium does more than keep your food clean"), kept **price-free** — the price isn't revealed until the ledger.
4. **What it costs — the payoff (moved here on purpose).** Only after the reader has seen the material and the 4-in-1 does the "running tab" ledger appear: five years of a cheap board (replacements + the separate sharpener, grater, second board) at ~$195, against TIBO at $59.99 once. The section transitions straight out of the previous one ("All of that, and it still comes out ahead on price…"), so price lands as a payoff instead of quoting price before desire. Then the **trust cluster** closes (4.8★/337 reviews, 50,000+ switched, 30-day guarantee, the offer strip) and the site footer ends the page. The old three-question FAQ dissolved into these beats.

**Why the second half exists.** The teardown alone is a case for *not keeping the cheap board*. The worth-it movement is the case for *wanting this one* — the material + features section (premium reframe and daily desire), the ledger (rational value), and the trust cluster (confidence to act).

This structure is a deliberate departure from the control/`-value` pages. **None of the old signature blocks appear** — no six-check scorecard table, no two-card price comparison, no star-rating pick card, no standalone green guarantee box. Proof is carried by narrative + one cited stat; the price argument by the ledger; the offer by an editorial recommendation + a compact offer strip.

**Honesty constraint:** BaseLocal did **not** physically test the boards. The page reviews published research, food-safety guidance, and the manufacturer's specs. The ledger figures are estimates, labeled "estimated at today's retail prices." Voice follows `general-writing-guide.md` (the #1 rule — *don't repeat yourself* — drove this rewrite): direct, warm, honest, story-driven; no banned words; zero em dashes.

Audience: BaseLocal's readership (73% female, 50+), arriving from ads or email on a microplastics/bacteria hook.

## 2. User flow

Single screen: `index.html`. No forms, no multi-step flow.

1. **Arrive** from the ad (which already explains plastic/microplastics + wood/bacteria and promises "see what they're using instead") → header, verdict headline (with the ad's 50,000+ figure carried through), hero image.
2. **Read the case** top to bottom — the page pulls straight down: the reveal (TIBO named immediately) → the evidence (health/food table) → the worth-it case (material + features in one section) → the ledger (what it costs, transitioning out of that section as a payoff) → trust cluster + recommendation + offer.
3. **Convert along the way, close at the offer strip** — inline CTAs after the evidence table, the Titanium block, and the features block let a convinced reader act mid-read; the price element carries its own CTA; the **offer strip is the single finishing CTA** (no separate bottom-CTA section). On mobile a sticky CTA also keeps "Check availability" one tap away. Any CTA opens the offer in a new tab (`target="_blank"`).
4. **Header disclosure** — an "Advertiser Disclosure" button in the header opens a dialog with BaseLocal's standard disclaimer (replicated from the live Welcome Page).

**Page section order:** header (brand lockup · **Advertiser Disclosure** tooltip) → front matter (dateline "Updated July 2026" above the H1 · H1 · three-panel hero banner with breathing room beneath; **top image fixed**, no divider) → **the reveal** (no header, two short beats: TIBO named and linked, the four tools inventoried) → **"What the research proves"** (source-roll intro → side-by-side evidence table → **inline CTA "See the TIBO board →"** → three H3 material blocks: Plastic / Wood / Titanium, each with short beats and a figure; the Titanium block closes with an **inline CTA**) → divider → **"TIBO's titanium does more than keep your food clean"** (the worth-it case — material and features merged: medical-grade metal, then the H3 **"Four tools, one motion"** with figure-pair, knives close, **inline CTA**, and the Marcus L. review card; price-free) → **"The best part: it's cheaper than the cheap boards"** (`#the-tab` — the combined price element: itemized five-year tab → $195 total → terracotta TIBO band with $59.99, savings line, and CTA) → divider → **"The people who switched aren't going back"** (trust-strip stat band + offer strip + guarantee line — **the page's finishing CTA is the offer strip**; there is no separate bottom-CTA section) → **site footer** (replicates baselocal.com's footer, carries the commission disclaimer). A **mobile-only sticky CTA** overlays the bottom of the viewport between the hero and the closing offer strip.

## 3. States

Static content page; no form states.

- **Default:** all content visible immediately. Only entrance motion is the hero block's staggered `fadeInUp` (H1/lead-in/banner at 0/80/160ms). Two small vanilla-JS behaviors: the disclosure popover and the mobile sticky CTA. No other scripting.
- **Advertiser Disclosure tooltip:** header `<button aria-haspopup="dialog" aria-expanded>` toggles a popover `<div role="dialog">` holding BaseLocal's three-paragraph disclaimer. Opens on click; closes on the × button, on outside click, and on Escape (which returns focus to the button). Anchored below the button, right-aligned, `width: min(340px, 88vw)` so it stays in the mobile viewport. Replicates the component on `baselocal.com/.../welcome/` (there a `<button>Advertiser Disclosure</button>` opening a dialog). Hidden by default (`[hidden]`), so it degrades to nothing if JS is off.
- **Reduced motion:** `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` collapses all animation/transitions to 0.01ms (the sticky CTA snaps rather than slides).
- **CTA hover/active:** primary buttons — Deep Terracotta + terracotta glow on hover, `scale(0.97)` on press. Button labels stay white (`.prose a.btn-primary` override). Links underline on hover.
- **Mobile sticky CTA (≤600px):** fixed bottom bar (~~$69.99~~ **$59.99** + "Get TIBO →") slides up once the hero scrolls off-screen, slides away once the **closing offer strip** enters view, so the two never stack. Hidden ≥601px. Tap target ≥44px. Passive `scroll`/`resize` listener toggling `.show`; degrades to hidden without JS.
- **Image failure:** every `<img>` carries descriptive alt text.
- **Edge cases:** `#two-costs` and `#the-tab` anchors must keep working if section order changes.
