Fig. 05 — Why it works The voice is the asset

Readers trust the recommendations because we never spend that trust cheaply.

Our writing guide bans hype outright: superlatives only when they're literally, defensibly true. Every paid item carries a disclosure asterisk. One over-hyped recommendation undoes months of credibility, so nothing gets over-hyped.

Restraint is the strategy. When we finally do recommend something, people listen, and click.

What we never write
✗"Jaw-dropping deal!"
✗"This hidden gem will blow your mind"
✗"You won't believe what's nearby"
✗"A total game-changer"
What runs instead
✓"The cheapest option on this list"
✓"Ask me how I know 🙃"
✓"$10k to a credit card — a true story"
✓"One we like, and here's why"
The left column is banned in our style guide. Literally. Source: BaseLocal general writing guide, 2026.

Some placements read like advice.

For a skeptical audience and a considered purchase, we write the placement as a tip from a knowledgeable friend: education first, offer second. Fewer readers click, but the ones who do arrive primed and convert.

Insurance covers disasters, not a $12k HVAC bill: A tree crashes through your roof, insurance writes the check. Your AC dies in the middle of summer, insurance says "that's maintenance."… See how they work (and which ones are worth it) →
The angle
A factual contrast, not a pitch
Two scenarios the reader already knows: the one insurance pays for, and the one it never will.
→
The register
"In case you didn't know…"
The copy explains how home warranties work before it links anywhere. Advice that happens to have a next step.
→
The trade
Fewer clicks, better clicks
Readers who click already know what they're shopping for. Conversion quality over raw volume, by design.

A women-owned brand and a menopause-sleep review, in front of a readership that's 73% women, most 50+.

The outcome

1,200+ unique clicks in a single morning, strong enough that a second campaign followed.

Doc. 15 — Working with us Structured per partnership

Start with a pilot. Decide with evidence.

Most newsletter advertising is built on one-off sends. We're built the other way: the pilot exists to find offers worth running for the long haul.

Deal structures are flexible: flat sponsorships, performance, hybrids. We'd rather fit the structure to the offer than quote a rate card, and the pilot tells both sides whether to scale.

We write the copy, run the variations, and share what we measure. You bring an offer worth recommending.
