Cirice

Restaurant

A tiny 6th Ave spot doing wood-fired everything; the Sunday porchetta is usually gone by seven, so go early or call ahead.

2716 6th Ave · ✓ checked Jul 14

"The oven came from a bakery in Spokane and the recipes came from a grandmother in Abruzzo. Six tables, one seating rhythm, and a porchetta that has sold out every Sunday since March. If you want a table without a wait, Tuesday is your friend."

From the Tacoma newsletter, Jul 14

Alma Mater

Music & café

Café by day, the best sightlines in the 253 by night — the old Elks lodge, reborn.

1322 Fawcett Ave · ✓ checked Jul 21

"The upstairs room reopened this month and the first shows sold out in a weekend. Downstairs, the café holds its own — order the miso caramel latte and take the window seat before the laptop crowd does."

The Fish Peddler

Seafood

Dockside seafood on the Foss — the crab roll travels well, the view doesn't have to.

"Order at the counter, take the dock seats, and let the seagulls know who's boss. The chowder is the sleeper pick — thick enough to hold a spoon upright, and they'll add smoked salmon if you ask nicely."
