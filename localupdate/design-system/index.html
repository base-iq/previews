The bulletin: black, white, and one electric interruption

Living visual reference for brain/brands/localupdate/design-system.md. Three governing rules: 90% of any surface is black on white (cobalt is rationed to the masthead slug, links, NEW flags, and one primary button per viewport); flat and square (radius 0, no shadows — depth comes from black rules and reversed blocks); it arrives, it doesn't animate (motion is a 120ms color change, nothing moves).

02 · Typography

One family, weight does the talking

Libre Franklin 400–900 is the entire voice; Courier Prime appears only as the stamp. Sentence case everywhere except slugs, kickers, stamps, flags, and the masthead. Headlines never exclaim — the weight is the volume.

03 · Logos

04 · Spacing · Rules · Depth

Flat and square

4px base scale. Radius is 0 everywhere — no exceptions, not even buttons. No shadows except shadow-overlay for elements genuinely floating (sticky bars, dropdowns). Depth comes from rules and reversed blocks.

The rule system

The tape — 5px Black. Opens every surface: top of page, top of email, above the bottom CTA.

1px Black — emphasis: date rows, byline rows, pull quotes, form section headers.

1px Rule gray — separation: list items, cards, table rows.

05 · Motion

It arrives, it doesn't animate

No entrances, no fades, no transforms, no parallax, no hover lifts. The single permitted transition: color (background, text, border, underline) at 120ms. A prefers-reduced-motion guard zeroes even that. This is the stillest system in the portfolio — stiller than AmericanAdvisor, which at least fades in.

13 · Email typography

Designed on the fallback: Helvetica & Courier

Gmail and Outlook render no web fonts. The email stack is Helvetica/Arial for everything, Courier New for stamps — the whole system degrades in one move. Franklin and Courier Prime ride along as progressive enhancement for Apple Mail. The masthead is always a PNG in email. Email body floor: 16px; nothing below 13px.

15 · Photography

News photos, not lifestyle stock

Candid, documentary, shot-on-the-scene register: the storefront that's opening, the road that's closed, the room where it was decided. Neutral color, higher contrast than the siblings' warm palettes — never filtered warm, never glossy. People 50+ appear as the capable subjects of the change, not as clip art. Every photo takes a stamp cutline. No illustration systems, no 3D renders, no emoji in editorial surfaces.
